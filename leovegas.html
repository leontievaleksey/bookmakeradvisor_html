How easy to the eye is the Leo Vegas Sports website?

The LeoVegas site is very much an old fashioned site with a content section which is not full width and is a main section on an image background and this is likely put off a lot of punters.

Their is no main sports menu and everything is contained in a top menu with the 3 main products Casino, Live Casino and Sports.

Each of these has a drop down and by clicking the sports drop down you will have a further menu with the 5 main sports. There is a further link on this menu to move through to other sports. This adds another click to the user journey so usability is something that can be improved.

The colour scheme of the site is made up of orange, blue and grey with black text. The font used is also a rounded font which makes it a little difficult to read.

Once you click through to an individual sport or event the interface improves. It is still sitting in a middle section but markets and prices are very nicely laid out and easy to see.

The site loads very quickly from our tests so this is a big positive compared to other chunkier sites. Also the layout and level of in event data and content has improved dramatically from our last review back in October 2017. Both football and horse racing now have excellent to to help the punter in bet selection.

Odds format & betting margin

Fractional and Decimal Fractional odds are exactly as its says on the tin…..a fraction. So lets take a price which is odds on, which might be 4/5 or four fifthes. So If you put a bet on of 1 pound, you will receive back your one pound stake plus four fifths, so a total of £1.80 LeoVegas Sports show their prices initially using Decimal odds which are very straightforward to understand, as all you need to do is take the decimal number, lets say 26.0 and deduct 1 which would give you odds of 25/1.
