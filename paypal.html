Can I use PayPal for Sports betting?

PayPal is one of the most convenient and easy ways to place bets online, which is why more and more sports betting sites are adding this as an accepted payment method. Customers have been using credit and debit cards to place bets online for decades, but with PayPal being a safer way to pay, it’s no wonder why millions are choosing to pay this way.

PayPal is a relatively new way of paying for goods and services, having only launched in 2002. It allows customers to store their payment details safely in one place and when it comes to placing bets, you log in and pay – that’s it.

Is it safe to use PayPal for UK Online betting sites?

There are many bookmakers that provide the option to deposit and withdraw using Paypal. Paypal is one of the most secure e-payment providers with accounts available in most world currencies.

One of the key benefits of using Paypal is that it can be linked to a debit or credit card. The other main advantage is that it is a very secure method of payment. Paypal has been operating for more than a decade and you can be sure your funds are safe.

How long does it take to deposit & withdraw funds using PayPal?

A really new benefit to users of PayPal is that you can now send money anywhere and to anybody immediately, even if the other person does not have a PayPal account. When making a withdrawal, It can take up to 24 hours for receive money from online bookmaker and another 2-3 days to receive money on your card or bank account

Are there any Fees for using PayPal at Online Bookies?

One thing you should check when using Paypal are the transaction fees, which can range between 2-5 % plus a small-added fee.

You should check inside your account. Also be aware of currency exchange rates, which will be lower with this payment option when you deposit or withdraw.

Conclusion on using PayPal as your deposit & withdrawal method on betting site

Payments for goods and services online has evolved greatly in the last 5 years, not only can you use your standard credit and debit card to fund your betting account, and of course bank transfer, but now you can also fund an account using an e-wallet, Western Union and very new to the bookmaking industry is the option to fund an account using Bitcoin.

As Bitcoin and other “Crypto” currencies gain pace in the financial world, you will see a lot more punters acting as traders. The opportunities on betting exchanges will be endless. Today we are looking at Paypal as an option for funding your account.
